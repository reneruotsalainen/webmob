Linux,

the open-source platform that runs the web.

Introduction

The majority of people use the term "Linux" to refer to a family of operating systems that are built around the Linux-kernel. These operating systems exist on all sorts of devices whether they're ARM or x86 based, super computers or embedded systems. Linux is mostly found in the smartphone and server worlds, but it is becoming more and more prevalent in the PC world too. Some of us are Linux users without even acknowledging it, if you are using an Android phone you are already using Linux. Fun fact: NASA's Perseverance Rover runs on Linux.

History

The Linux kernel was created in 1991 by Linus Torvalds, out of frustration with the licencing of operating systems available back then. According to the creator, the project started as a hobby that eventually unfolded into an operating system kernel.
